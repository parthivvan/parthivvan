### 🏥 Vaidya-AI — *Healthcare AI Copilot*
Most health information online is either too clinical or dangerously oversimplified. Vaidya-AI bridges that gap.

An AI assistant that helps users understand medical conditions, interpret symptoms, and navigate healthcare options — built with LLM orchestration and a RAG layer over structured health data. Not a chatbot. A reasoning system that knows when to be careful.

`Python` `LangChain` `RAG` `Vector DB` `FastAPI`

---

### 🔬 ResearchAI & ResearchAI-2.0 — *Research Automation Engine*
Academic research has a discovery problem. Relevant papers get missed. Synthesis takes weeks. Researchers spend 40% of their time just finding information.

ResearchAI automates the most painful parts: semantic paper search, contextual summarization, and knowledge organization — built with LangChain pipelines and vector-based semantic retrieval. Version 2.0 improved retrieval accuracy and added multi-document synthesis.

`JavaScript` `LangChain` `Vector Search` `Semantic Retrieval`

→ [ResearchAI](https://github.com/parthivvan/ResearchAI) · [ResearchAI-2.0](https://github.com/parthivvan/ResearchAI-2.0)

### 🔒 Site Security Analyzer — *Automated Web Security Assessment*
Security posture assessment shouldn't require a dedicated security team. This tool runs automated checks against common vulnerabilities, identifies misconfigurations, and surfaces actionable fixes — designed for developers who need security insights without a $50K audit.

### 🔍 ClaimLens — *AI Claims Intelligence*
Insurance and legal claims are dense, jargon-heavy, and deliberately hard to parse. ClaimLens uses LLM reasoning to extract meaning, flag anomalies, and surface what matters in complex claim documents.

`TypeScript` `LLM Reasoning` `Document AI`

→ [ClaimLens](https://github.com/parthivvan/ClaimLens)

### 🏛️ Jan-Sahayak & Vesta-Scheme-Finder — *Government Services, Made Accessible*
India has hundreds of government welfare schemes. Most eligible citizens never claim them because discovering and navigating them is a bureaucratic nightmare.

These two projects tackle that: automated scheme matching, eligibility filtering, and plain-language explanations — built for people who've been failed by complexity.

`Python` `NLP` `Automation`

→ [Jan-Sahayak](https://github.com/parthivvan/Jan-Sahayak)
